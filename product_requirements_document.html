You can elaborate on each section for a full PRD or summarize for a concise project brief. --- **[PROJECT NAME] - Product Requirements Document (PRD) / Project Brief** **Document Version:** 1.0 **Date:** [Current Date] **Author(s):** [Your Name/Team] **Status:** [Draft / Under Review / Approved] --- ### 1. Executive Summary * **Purpose:** A high-level overview of the project, its goals, and expected impact. It should be digestible in 1-2 minutes. * **Content:** Briefly state the problem, the proposed solution, the primary objective, and the key benefits. --- ### 2. Problem Statement * **Purpose:** Clearly define the pain point, opportunity, or challenge this project aims to address. * **Content:** * **What is the current situation?** Describe the status quo. * **What is the undesirable outcome or unmet need?** Quantify the problem if possible (e.g., "users abandon checkout at 25%," "support tickets related to X are up 15%"). * **Who is affected?** (Users, business, internal teams). * **Why is this a problem worth solving now?** (Urgency, strategic alignment). --- ### 3. Goals & Objectives * **Purpose:** Define what success looks like for this project. Should be SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound). * **Content:** * **Overarching Goal:** The main desired outcome. * **Specific Objectives (KPIs):** Concrete metrics that will indicate success. * *Example:* "Increase user engagement (weekly active users) by 10% within 3 months post-launch." * *Example:* "Reduce customer support tickets related to [issue] by 20%." * *Example:* "Improve conversion rate for [feature] by 5%." --- ### 4. Target Audience / Users * **Purpose:** Clearly identify who will benefit from or use this product/feature. * **Content:** * **Primary Users:** Who are they? (e.g., "First-time users," "Existing power users," "Small business owners"). * **User Personas (brief description):** What are their key characteristics, needs, and pain points relevant to this project? * **Secondary Users (if any):** E.g., internal teams, administrators. --- ### 5. Proposed Solution & Key Features * **Purpose:** Describe the high-level solution and the core functionalities required. * **Content:** * **High-Level Solution Concept:** Briefly explain how the problem will be solved. * **Key Features (User Stories/Epics):** List the main functionalities. Focus on *what* it does, not necessarily *how* it's built yet. * *Example:* "As a new user, I want to be guided through the initial setup process so I can start using the product quickly." * *Example:* "As a system admin, I want to be able to reset user passwords securely." * **User Flows / Experience Highlights (Optional):** Briefly describe the core user journey or key interactions. --- ### 6. Scope (In & Out) * **Purpose:** Clearly define what is included in this project and, crucially, what is explicitly *not* included. This helps manage expectations and prevent scope creep. * **Content:** * **In Scope:** List the features, functionalities, and user groups that are part of this initial release/project. * **Out of Scope:** List items that might seem related but are *not* part of this project. (e.g., "Advanced analytics dashboard," "Support for IE11," "Internationalization"). * **Future Considerations / Phases (Optional):** Briefly mention potential follow-up work. --- ### 7. Success Metrics & KPIs (Detailed) * **Purpose:** Reiterate and detail the specific metrics that will be used to track project performance post-launch. (Often a more detailed version of Section 3). * **Content:** * **Metric 1:** [Name] * **Baseline:** [Current Value] * **Target:** [Desired Value] * **Measurement Method:** [How will it be measured?] * **Metric 2:** ... * **Impact:** How does achieving these targets tie back to the overall business goals? --- ### 8. Assumptions, Constraints & Risks * **Purpose:** Document factors that influence the project and potential challenges. * **Content:** * **Assumptions:** Things we believe to be true but haven't necessarily proven yet (e.g., "Users will understand the new UI without extensive tutorials," "API integration will be straightforward"). * **Constraints:** Limitations or restrictions (e.g., "Fixed budget of X," "Launch by Y date," "Must integrate with legacy system Z," "Limited engineering resources"). * **Risks:** Potential problems that could hinder success (e.g., "Low user adoption," "Technical complexity leads to delays," "Competitor launches similar feature"). * **Mitigation Strategies (Optional):** How will we address these risks? --- ### 9. Technical Considerations (High-Level) * **Purpose:** Provide initial thoughts on technical requirements and potential challenges for engineering. * **Content:** * **Integrations:** Any external systems or APIs required. * **Performance:** High-level expectations (e.g., "Pages must load within 2 seconds"). * **Security:** Any specific security requirements. * **Scalability:** Any anticipated load or growth that needs to be factored in. * **Platform Support:** Web, iOS, Android, specific browsers, etc. --- ### 10. Dependencies * **Purpose:** Identify other teams, projects, or external factors that this project relies on. * **Content:** * **Internal Teams:** Marketing, Sales, Legal, Support, other Product Teams. * **External Partners:** Vendors, third-party services. * **Other Projects:** If this project needs another project to complete first. --- ### 11. Rollout Plan (High-Level) * **Purpose:** Briefly outline the strategy for launching the product/feature. * **Content:** * **Phased Rollout?** (e.g., "Alpha > Beta > GA," "Geographic rollout"). * **Marketing & Communications:** How will users be informed? * **Support & Training:** What's needed for internal teams? --- ### 12. Open Questions & Next Steps * **Purpose:** Acknowledge areas that still need clarification and define immediate actions. * **Content:** * **Unanswered Questions:** (e.g., "What is the exact pricing model?", "Can we use [specific technology]?"). * **Immediate Next Steps:** (e.g., "Schedule design kickoff," "Conduct user research on X," "Finalize technical architecture review"). --- ### 13. Appendices (Optional) * **Purpose:** Include supporting documentation. * **Content:** * User Research Findings * Competitive Analysis * Mockups/Wireframes * Technical Designs * Legal/Compliance Documents --- --- ## Part 2: Generic Example PRD/Brief (Improving User Onboarding) Here’s an example of how you might fill out the template for a hypothetical project: --- **Project Name: User Onboarding Experience Improvement** **Document Version:** 1.0 **Date:** October 26, 2023 **Author(s):** Alex Product Manager **Status:** Draft --- ### 1. Executive Summary This project aims to redesign and enhance our platform's user onboarding experience. The current onboarding process leads to significant drop-off, impacting our new user activation rate. By implementing an interactive tutorial, clear progress indicators, and personalized initial setup, we expect to significantly improve user activation and retention, contributing directly to our growth objectives. --- ### 2. Problem Statement Our current user onboarding process is overly complex and lacks clear guidance, resulting in a **35% drop-off rate** during the first 24 hours post-signup. New users frequently report confusion about how to get started, leading to frustration and reduced product adoption. This directly impacts our monthly active user (MAU) growth and increases customer support inquiries related to basic setup by **15% month-over-month**. --- ### 3. Goals & Objectives * **Overarching Goal:** Increase new user activation and reduce early churn by providing a seamless and intuitive onboarding experience. * **Specific Objectives:** * Increase the **7-day activation rate** (users completing primary setup actions) from 45% to **60%** within 3 months of launch. * Reduce new user **churn within the first 30 days** from 20% to **15%** within 6 months of launch. * Decrease support tickets related to "getting started" issues by **25%** within 3 months of launch. --- ### 4. Target Audience / Users * **Primary Users:** * **New Sign-ups:** Individuals who have just created an account and are experiencing the product for the first time. * *Persona Example:* "Sarah, a small business owner looking for an efficient project management tool. She's tech-savvy but time-poor and needs to see value quickly." * **Secondary Users:** * Customer Support Team (will handle fewer "how-to" questions). * Sales Team (will have more engaged trial users to convert). --- ### 5. Proposed Solution & Key Features We will introduce a guided, interactive onboarding flow designed to educate users, personalize their experience, and prompt completion of critical setup actions. * **High-Level Solution Concept:** A step-by-step, personalized onboarding wizard integrated directly into the product experience, replacing the current static "welcome email." * **Key Features:** * **Interactive Product Tour:** A guided, dismissible tour highlighting key UI elements and functionalities based on initial user intent. * *Example User Story:* "As a new user, I want an interactive tour that shows me where the key features are, so I can learn how to navigate the product." * **Progress Indicator:** A visual progress bar or checklist showing onboarding completion status. * *Example User Story:* "As a new user, I want to see my onboarding progress so I know how much more I need to do to get started." * **Personalized Setup Wizard:** Questions at signup to tailor the initial experience (e.g., "What is your primary goal?", "What industry are you in?"). * *Example User Story:* "As a new user, I want to answer a few questions about my needs so the product can suggest relevant features and templates." * **"Quick Start" Templates/Demos:** Pre-populated data or templates relevant to the user's selected industry/goal. * *Example User Story:* "As a small business owner, I want a pre-built project template so I can immediately start managing my first project without manual setup." * **Contextual Help & Tooltips:** In-app mini-tutorials or tooltips that appear when users encounter specific features for the first time. --- ### 6. Scope (In & Out) * **In Scope:** * Redesign of the initial post-signup experience on the web application. * Development of interactive product tours, progress indicators, and personalized setup logic. * Integration with existing user profile and data systems. * A/B testing framework for onboarding variations. * **Out of Scope:** * Onboarding experience for mobile applications (iOS/Android). * Comprehensive in-app help documentation or a full knowledge base. * Automated email drip campaigns specific to onboarding (Marketing will handle this separately). * Advanced analytics dashboards for power users. * **Future Considerations:** Mobile onboarding, localized onboarding for international users, integration with external CRM for sales follow-up. --- ### 7. Success Metrics & KPIs (Detailed) * **Metric 1: 7-Day Activation Rate** * **Definition:** Percentage of users who complete 3 core setup actions (e.g., create first project, invite team member, connect integration) within 7 days of signup. * **Baseline:** 45% * **Target:** 60% * **Measurement Method:** Tracked via Amplitude/Mixpanel, reported weekly. * **Metric 2: 30-Day Churn Rate** * **Definition:** Percentage of activated users who become inactive (no logins or product usage) within 30 days of signup. * **Baseline:** 20% * **Target:** 15% * **Measurement Method:** Tracked via internal analytics, reported monthly. * **Metric 3: Support Tickets related to "Getting Started"** * **Definition:** Volume of support tickets categorized as "Onboarding" or "Getting Started." * **Baseline:** ~100 tickets/month * **Target:** Reduce to 75 tickets/month * **Measurement Method:** Tracked via Zendesk, reported weekly by Support Lead. --- ### 8. Assumptions, Constraints & Risks * **Assumptions:** * User data collected during signup (e.g., industry, role) is reliable and sufficient for initial personalization. * Our internal analytics infrastructure can accurately track new activation events. * The Engineering team has the bandwidth to implement the proposed changes within the desired timeline. * **Constraints:** * **Timeline:** Must launch initial V1 by end of Q1 2024 to support new marketing campaigns. * **Resources:** Limited to 1 FE, 1 BE engineer, 1 Designer, 0.5 QA for the duration of the project. * **Risks:** * **Low User Adoption of New Flow:** Users might still skip the guided tour. * *Mitigation:* Implement mandatory (but dismissible) steps, prominent calls-to-action, A/B test different entry points. * **Technical Complexity/Delays:** Integrating new UI elements and personalization logic might be more complex than anticipated. * *Mitigation:* Conduct early technical spike, break down features into smaller, manageable chunks. * **Negative Impact on Edge Cases:** The personalized flow might not cater well to niche user segments. * *Mitigation:* Include an "I'll explore on my own" option, conduct targeted user testing with diverse user groups. --- ### 9. Technical Considerations (High-Level) * **Integrations:** Requires integration with our existing user authentication and profile services. Potential for future integration with Salesforce (CRM) for lead qualification. * **Performance:** The onboarding flow must load quickly and be highly responsive to avoid user frustration. * **Security:** Ensure secure handling of any personalized data collected. * **Scalability:** The solution must scale to support our projected user growth of 20% MoM. * **Platform Support:** Web application only (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge - current versions). --- ### 10. Dependencies * **Marketing Team:** For aligning messaging in welcome emails and initial user communications. * **Data/Analytics Team:** For setting up new event tracking and dashboard reporting for KPIs. * **Customer Support Team:** For training on the new onboarding flow and updating FAQs. * **Legal Team:** To review any new data collection points for compliance. --- ### 11. Rollout Plan (High-Level) * **Phase 1 (Alpha):** Internal team testing and feedback (2 weeks). * **Phase 2 (Beta):** Gradual rollout to 10% of new sign-ups, monitored closely for feedback and metrics (3-4 weeks). * **Phase 3 (GA):** Full public launch to 100% of new sign-ups, followed by ongoing A/B testing and optimization. * **Marketing & Communications:** Blog post, in-app announcement, updated help docs. * **Support & Training:** Internal session for support agents, updated internal knowledge base. --- ### 12. Open Questions & Next Steps * **Open Questions:** * What is the maximum number of steps we deem acceptable for the personalized setup wizard before it becomes tedious? * How will we handle users who abandon the onboarding mid-way and return later? * What level of "gamification" (e.g., badges for completion) should be explored? * **Immediate Next Steps:** * Schedule Design Kickoff meeting for detailed wireframing and prototyping. * Technical discovery session with Engineering Lead to validate feasibility and identify critical path items. * User research on existing onboarding pain points (if not already completed). --- ### 13. Appendices (Optional) * User Survey Results - "New User Frustrations" (October 2023) * Competitive Onboarding Analysis (Miro, Asana, Slack) * Initial Wireframe Sketches (V0.1) ---
